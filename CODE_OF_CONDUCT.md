# Code of Conduct

## The short version

Be respectful, assume good faith, and remember there's a person on the other end of every issue, PR, and
review comment. Disagreements about lore, data, or code are normal and welcome — personal attacks,
harassment, and bad-faith arguing are not.

## Expected

- Give and receive feedback about the *work*, not the *person*.
- Back up factual disagreements with a source when you can.
- Assume people are contributing in good faith, especially newcomers still learning the project's
  conventions.
- Respect maintainer decisions on scope and direction, even when you'd have chosen differently — you're
  always welcome to fork.

## Not okay

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks, in any project space (issues, PRs, discussions, commit
  messages).
- Discriminatory jokes, language, or imagery.
- Doxxing or sharing anyone's private information without consent.
- Sustained disruption of discussions, or deliberately derailing issues/PRs off-topic.
- Submitting content that violates the [image contribution guidelines](CONTRIBUTING.md#contributing-images)
  after being asked not to.

## Scope

This applies to every space connected to the project: the GitHub repo (issues, pull requests,
discussions, commits) and any other channel the maintainers designate in the future.

## Enforcement

Report a concern by opening a private security advisory on the repo, or by emailing the maintainer
directly if that's not available to you. Reports are handled confidentially. Maintainers may edit,
remove, or lock content, and may warn, temporarily restrict, or ban anyone whose behavior violates this
Code of Conduct, at their discretion.
